# Archetype Visual System

Sources: Tillman (Creative Character Design), Mark & Pearson (The Hero and the Outlaw), Bancroft (Creating Characters with Personality), 3dtotal (Fundamentals of Character Design)

This file bridges archetype selection (character-discovery.md) and visual execution (shape-color-theory.md). It translates the 12 Jungian archetypes into concrete visual specifications — shape language, color direction, silhouette, proportion, costume, and movement. Use it to make design decisions that feel psychologically coherent rather than arbitrary.

---

## 1. How Archetypes Drive Visual Design

Every character communicates personality before speaking a word. Shape, color, and silhouette are the primary carriers of that signal. Personality dimensions (motivation, fear, wound, moral code) map to an archetype, which maps to a visual register (shape language, color temperature, silhouette weight, movement quality).

The four archetype quadrants carry shared visual tendencies:

| Quadrant | Core Motivation | Archetypes | Visual Register |
|----------|----------------|------------|-----------------|
| **Independence** | Freedom, mastery, self-expression | Hero, Explorer, Outlaw, Creator | High contrast, dynamic diagonals, strong silhouettes |
| **Belonging** | Connection, love, community | Everyman, Lover, Jester, Caregiver | Warm palettes, rounded forms, open posture |
| **Stability** | Safety, order, control | Ruler, Caregiver, Sage, Innocent | Structured proportions, grounded stances, restrained palettes |
| **Change** | Transformation, disruption, magic | Magician, Creator, Explorer, Outlaw | Expressive shapes, unexpected color, asymmetric silhouettes |

Archetypes at quadrant intersections (Caregiver spans Belonging/Stability; Creator spans Independence/Change) draw from both registers and produce the most visually complex characters.

## 2. The 12 Archetypes — Complete Visual DNA

### Hero

**Core drive:** Prove worth through courage and decisive action. Protect others by overcoming the obstacle.
**Character role:** Protagonist, champion, warrior, athlete, leader under pressure.

**Shape language:** Dominant squares and upward-pointing triangles. Broad shoulders taper to a stable base. Angular jaw, strong brow ridge. No soft curves in the primary silhouette — softness reads as vulnerability the Hero has not yet earned.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#CC2200` | Warm red — courage, urgency, blood |
| Secondary | `#1A2E4A` | Deep navy — discipline, duty |
| Accent | `#E8A020` | Burnished gold — achievement, honor |
| Neutral | `#2A2A2A` | Near black — intensity, focus |

**Silhouette:** Tall to heroic proportions. Wide shoulders, narrow waist, planted stance. Distinctive feature: reads as a shield shape when arms are at rest.

**Proportion guidance:** 8–9 head heights (heroic scale). Shoulder width equals 3 head widths. Arms slightly exaggerated.

**Costume direction:** Functional over decorative. Worn, battle-tested gear signals experience. Insignia or emblem on chest or shoulder. Condition: weathered but maintained — the Hero respects their tools.

**Movement quality:** Purposeful, direct, economical. Weight shifts forward before action. Stillness reads as readiness.

**Example characters:** Superman, Mulan, Achilles, Katniss Everdeen

**AI prompt hints:** "broad-shouldered warrior, angular jaw, worn armor with insignia, forward-leaning stance" / "heroic proportions, square silhouette, warm red and gold palette, determined expression"

### Sage / Mentor

**Core drive:** Understand the truth and share it. Guide others toward wisdom they cannot yet see.
**Character role:** Mentor, scholar, advisor, elder, oracle.

**Shape language:** Dominant squares with softened corners. Grounded, stable base. Vertical emphasis in costume (robes, tall collars) suggests accumulated knowledge. Rounded shoulders signal approachability over aggression.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#2C4A6E` | Deep slate blue — depth, authority |
| Secondary | `#5C4A2A` | Warm brown — earth, rootedness |
| Accent | `#C9A84C` | Aged gold — wisdom, credibility |
| Neutral | `#8A8A7A` | Warm grey — neutrality, age |

**Silhouette:** Medium height, slightly stooped or settled. Wider through the middle — the Sage has stopped performing physical dominance. Distinctive feature: hands visible and expressive; the Sage gestures when speaking.

**Proportion guidance:** 7–8 head heights (realistic to slightly stylized). Proportions feel settled rather than dynamic. Head slightly larger relative to body in stylized versions — knowledge lives in the mind.

**Costume direction:** Layered, worn, functional. Books, scrolls, or tools of their discipline visible. Condition: well-used, not neglected. Color often muted — the Sage's appearance does not compete with their words.

**Movement quality:** Deliberate, unhurried. Weight settled into the ground. Gestures precise and intentional. The Sage does not fidget.

**Example characters:** Gandalf, Yoda, Dumbledore, Chiron

**AI prompt hints:** "robed elder with layered garments, expressive hands, warm earth tones and aged gold" / "settled posture, slightly stooped, scholar's tools visible, deep blue and brown palette"

### Innocent

**Core drive:** Experience the world as safe, good, and full of wonder. Preserve purity.
**Character role:** Child, idealist, optimist, the one who believes when others have stopped.

**Shape language:** Pure circles dominate. Round face, soft cheeks, large eyes. No sharp angles anywhere in the primary design. Even clothing has rounded hems and soft folds.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#F5F0E8` | Warm cream — purity, morning light |
| Secondary | `#A8D4E8` | Soft sky blue — openness, calm |
| Accent | `#E8C87A` | Warm gold — gentle optimism |
| Supporting | `#D4EAD0` | Mint green — freshness, nature |

**Silhouette:** Small to medium height. Soft, rounded outline with no hard edges. Distinctive feature: wide eyes that take up a large proportion of the face — the Innocent sees everything as new.

**Proportion guidance:** 5–6 head heights (stylized) or 6–7 (realistic young character). Head large relative to body. Limbs slightly short and soft.

**Costume direction:** Simple, clean, unadorned. Light colors, natural fabrics. Condition: clean but not precious — the Innocent plays in the world. No armor, no weapons, no symbols of power.

**Movement quality:** Light, bouncy, spontaneous. Weight rises onto the balls of the feet. Gestures open and expansive.

**Example characters:** Totoro, Cinderella (before the ball), Forrest Gump, Amélie

**AI prompt hints:** "round soft face, large eyes, cream and sky blue palette, simple clean clothing" / "childlike proportions, circular silhouette, open posture, warm gentle colors"

### Explorer

**Core drive:** Discover what lies beyond the known. Authentic experience over comfort.
**Character role:** Wanderer, scout, adventurer, the one who leaves and returns changed.

**Shape language:** Mix of triangles (forward motion, direction) and organic irregular forms (the natural world). Asymmetric details — one strap, a worn patch, a tool hanging at an angle — signal a life lived in motion.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#7A4A1E` | Terracotta — earth, endurance |
| Secondary | `#2D5016` | Forest green — nature, survival |
| Accent | `#C4622D` | Burnt orange — energy, horizon |
| Neutral | `#C4A882` | Sand — openness, distance |

**Silhouette:** Lean, wiry, built for distance not power. Slightly asymmetric from gear and habit. Distinctive feature: the silhouette suggests readiness to move — weight never fully settled.

**Proportion guidance:** 7–8 head heights. Lean build, long legs relative to torso. Slightly weathered features.

**Costume direction:** Functional, layered for changing conditions. Worn and repaired rather than replaced. Maps, tools, or navigation instruments visible. Condition: well-used, patched, authentic.

**Movement quality:** Efficient, scanning. Eyes move before the body. Always oriented toward the horizon; walks with purpose even without a destination.

**Example characters:** Indiana Jones, Moana, Bilbo Baggins, Lara Croft

**AI prompt hints:** "lean weathered traveler, asymmetric gear, terracotta and forest green, horizon-scanning posture" / "explorer's kit, worn layered clothing, lean silhouette, earthy palette"

### Outlaw / Rebel

**Core drive:** Disrupt the system that causes harm. Freedom through destruction of false order.
**Character role:** Anti-hero, revolutionary, outcast, the one who breaks rules to expose them.

**Shape language:** Dominant triangles, especially inverted or asymmetric. Sharp edges, broken lines, deliberate asymmetry. The Outlaw's design violates the visual rules that other characters follow.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#0D0D0D` | Near black — defiance, raw power |
| Secondary | `#8B0000` | Deep red — danger, passion |
| Accent | `#CCFF00` | Acid yellow-green — provocation, disruption |
| Neutral | `#4A4A4A` | Raw grey — industrial, unfinished |

**Silhouette:** Deliberately unconventional — lean and angular or heavy and imposing, but always breaking the expected visual grammar of the world they inhabit. Distinctive feature: asymmetric costume elements, visible damage or modification.

**Proportion guidance:** Variable. Exaggerate whatever quality makes them threatening or transgressive in context.

**Costume direction:** Modified, repurposed, or deliberately anti-establishment. Symbols of the system they reject, worn ironically or defaced. Condition: deliberately rough — the Outlaw does not perform respectability.

**Movement quality:** Unpredictable, coiled. Stillness that reads as threat rather than patience. Moves through space as if the rules of that space do not apply to them.

**Example characters:** V (V for Vendetta), Tyler Durden, Zorro, Harley Quinn

**AI prompt hints:** "asymmetric angular silhouette, near black and acid green, deliberately broken visual rules, coiled threatening posture" / "rebel costume with defaced symbols, sharp triangular forms, high contrast dark palette"

### Magician

**Core drive:** Transform reality. Make the impossible manifest through mastery of hidden forces.
**Character role:** Wizard, alchemist, visionary, the one who changes what others accept as fixed.

**Shape language:** Circles and spirals (transformation, cycles, hidden depth). Shapes that suggest dual readings or hidden meaning. Flowing forms that suggest energy in motion.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#2D1B69` | Deep purple — mystery, transformation |
| Secondary | `#0D1B3E` | Midnight blue — depth, possibility |
| Accent | `#C9A84C` | Gold — achievement, revelation |
| Supporting | `#1A6B5A` | Deep teal — hidden knowledge |

**Silhouette:** Tall, flowing, slightly otherworldly. Garments that move independently of the body suggest forces beyond the physical. Distinctive feature: hands always prominent — the Magician's power flows through gesture.

**Proportion guidance:** 8–9 head heights, elongated. Slightly unreal proportions reinforce that the Magician operates by different rules.

**Costume direction:** Layered with symbolic elements — stars, runes, geometric patterns. Materials that catch light differently (iridescent, metallic, deep velvet). Condition: immaculate or deliberately mysterious — never simply worn.

**Movement quality:** Fluid, deliberate, slightly outside normal rhythm. Pauses at unexpected moments. Gestures seem to shape the air.

**Example characters:** Merlin, Doctor Strange, Elsa, Prospero

**AI prompt hints:** "flowing robes with symbolic patterns, elongated proportions, deep purple and gold, hands prominent in gesture" / "otherworldly silhouette, iridescent or velvet materials, circular motifs, midnight blue palette"

### Lover

**Core drive:** Achieve deep connection and sensory richness. Beauty and intimacy as the highest goods.
**Character role:** Romantic lead, aesthete, devoted companion, the one who feels most intensely.

**Shape language:** Flowing curves, organic forms, nothing angular or rigid. The Lover's silhouette is defined by graceful lines that suggest movement even in stillness.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#8B1A3A` | Deep rose-burgundy — passion, depth |
| Secondary | `#F2C4CE` | Blush pink — softness, intimacy |
| Accent | `#C9A87A` | Champagne gold — luxury, warmth |
| Neutral | `#FAF0E6` | Warm cream — sensuality, comfort |

**Silhouette:** Graceful, flowing, defined by curves. Costume elements that move — flowing fabric, loose hair. Distinctive feature: reads as open and inviting rather than guarded.

**Proportion guidance:** 7–8 head heights. Proportions emphasize grace over power. Features refined and expressive.

**Costume direction:** Beautiful, tactile, chosen for sensory pleasure as much as function. Rich fabrics, warm colors, decorative elements that reward close attention. Condition: carefully maintained — the Lover honors beauty.

**Movement quality:** Fluid, expressive, emotionally present. Weight shifts communicate feeling. The body responds to the emotional temperature of every scene.

**Example characters:** Romeo, Isolde, Jay Gatsby, Amélie

**AI prompt hints:** "flowing graceful silhouette, deep rose and champagne gold, rich fabric with movement, emotionally expressive face" / "curved organic forms, warm intimate palette, refined features, open inviting posture"

### Jester / Trickster

**Core drive:** Expose truth through play. Disrupt through laughter what cannot be dismantled through force.
**Character role:** Comic relief, chaos agent, the fool who speaks truth to power, the one who reveals by breaking rules.

**Shape language:** Circles and triangles in deliberate tension — the Jester combines the approachable (circle) with the disruptive (triangle). Asymmetric, bouncy, deliberately unstable compositions.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#FFD700` | Bright yellow — joy, energy, attention |
| Secondary | `#FF4500` | Vivid orange-red — excitement, mischief |
| Accent | `#00BFFF` | Electric blue — surprise, wit |
| Neutral | `#FFFFFF` | White — clarity, bounce |

**Silhouette:** Energetic, unstable, mid-motion. The Jester is rarely fully at rest — even standing still, the silhouette suggests imminent movement. Distinctive feature: exaggerated extremities (large hands, expressive feet) that amplify gesture.

**Proportion guidance:** 5–7 head heights (stylized). Exaggerated features — large eyes, expressive mouth, elastic face. Limbs slightly rubbery.

**Costume direction:** Colorful, mismatched, deliberately incongruous. Elements that jingle, flap, or draw attention. Condition: chaotic but intentional — the Jester's disorder is curated.

**Movement quality:** Unpredictable, elastic, physically expressive. Exaggerated reactions. Uses the full range of motion. The Jester's body is a performance instrument.

**Example characters:** Loki, The Joker (sympathetic readings), Puck, Deadpool

**AI prompt hints:** "elastic exaggerated proportions, bright yellow and electric blue, mid-motion silhouette, mismatched colorful costume" / "trickster energy, large expressive hands, bouncy unstable stance, high-contrast playful palette"

### Everyman

**Core drive:** Belong. Be accepted as part of something real without pretension or performance.
**Character role:** Audience surrogate, relatable protagonist, the ordinary person in extraordinary circumstances.

**Shape language:** Balanced mix of circles and squares — approachable but grounded. No extreme shapes. The Everyman's design is deliberately unremarkable in isolation; they read as real.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#3A6EA5` | Mid blue — honest, reliable |
| Secondary | `#8B7355` | Warm brown — authenticity, groundedness |
| Accent | `#E87722` | Friendly orange — warmth, accessibility |
| Neutral | `#F5F0E8` | Warm off-white — unpretentious |

**Silhouette:** Average height, average build. Nothing exaggerated. Distinctive feature: the Everyman's silhouette is defined by what it lacks — no dramatic cape, no imposing armor, no otherworldly proportions.

**Proportion guidance:** 6–7 head heights (realistic). Proportions feel accurate to real human variation. Features specific and individual rather than idealized.

**Costume direction:** Practical, worn, personal. Clothes that have history — a favorite jacket, worn-in shoes. Condition: lived-in, not neglected. The Everyman's costume tells a story of actual use.

**Movement quality:** Natural, unperformed. Comfortable stillness. Reactions feel genuine rather than theatrical.

**Example characters:** Sam Gamgee, Katniss Everdeen (early), Peter Parker, Erin Brockovich

**AI prompt hints:** "average proportions, lived-in practical clothing, warm blue and brown palette, genuine unperformed posture" / "relatable realistic design, specific individual features, no exaggeration, honest warm colors"

### Caregiver

**Core drive:** Protect and nurture others. Place others' wellbeing above personal safety or comfort.
**Character role:** Healer, parent, protector, the one who holds the group together through sacrifice.

**Shape language:** Dominant circles and soft squares. Rounded, embracing forms. The Caregiver's silhouette suggests enclosure and protection — arms that curve inward, a posture that creates space for others.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#4A9B6F` | Soft green — growth, healing |
| Secondary | `#5B8DB8` | Warm blue — calm, trust |
| Accent | `#E8A598` | Gentle pink — warmth, compassion |
| Neutral | `#FAF5EE` | Warm cream — comfort, safety |

**Silhouette:** Medium height, slightly soft through the middle — the Caregiver's body is not optimized for combat. Distinctive feature: arms and hands positioned to receive or protect rather than strike.

**Proportion guidance:** 6–7 head heights. Proportions feel warm and approachable. Features expressive and empathetic — the Caregiver's face communicates concern and attention.

**Costume direction:** Practical, functional, chosen for the work of caring. Pockets, tools of their trade, evidence of recent use. Condition: clean but working — the Caregiver does not prioritize their own appearance.

**Movement quality:** Attentive, responsive to others. Moves toward distress rather than away. Stillness is watchful. Always oriented toward whoever needs them.

**Example characters:** Samwise Gamgee, Molly Weasley, Atticus Finch, Baymax

**AI prompt hints:** "soft rounded silhouette, arms in receiving posture, gentle green and blue palette, warm attentive expression" / "caregiver's tools visible, practical clothing, embracing body language, muted warm colors"

### Creator

**Core drive:** Make something of enduring value and originality. Express the inner vision in the outer world.
**Character role:** Artist, inventor, craftsperson, the one who builds what did not exist before.

**Shape language:** Intentional and distinctive — the Creator's design is defined by deliberate choice rather than a fixed shape family. May be geometric precision or organic expressiveness, but every element is chosen. Tools and materials of their craft are integrated into the silhouette.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#1A1A1A` | Near black — craft, precision, depth |
| Secondary | `#F5F5F0` | Near white — space, possibility |
| Accent | `#E84020` | Vivid red — creative energy, passion |
| Supporting | `#4A7A9B` | Considered blue — intentionality |

**Silhouette:** Defined by their work — ink-stained hands, tools at the belt, materials in progress. Distinctive feature: the Creator's silhouette includes evidence of making; they are never separate from their craft.

**Proportion guidance:** 7–8 head heights. Proportions serve the character's specific creative discipline. A sculptor reads differently from a composer — let the craft shape the body.

**Costume direction:** Functional for their specific creative work. Evidence of process — paint, clay, sawdust, code. Condition: deliberately worn in the service of making. The Creator's appearance is a byproduct of their work, not a performance.

**Movement quality:** Absorbed, precise when working, expansive when inspired. Hands always active — touching, measuring, shaping.

**Example characters:** Tony Stark, Hephaestus, Frida Kahlo, Willy Wonka

**AI prompt hints:** "craftsperson's tools integrated into silhouette, evidence of making on clothing, near black and vivid red accent, absorbed focused posture" / "inventor's workshop aesthetic, precise hands, materials visible, intentional distinctive design"

### Ruler

**Core drive:** Establish and maintain order. Lead through authority, vision, and the power to compel.
**Character role:** King, CEO, general, the one who sets the terms by which others operate.

**Shape language:** Dominant squares and strong verticals. Symmetrical, formal, structured. The Ruler's design communicates that they occupy the center of their world — everything else organizes around them.

**Color palette direction**

| Role | Hex | Rationale |
|------|-----|-----------|
| Primary | `#0A1628` | Deep navy — authority, permanence |
| Secondary | `#1A1A1A` | Near black — power, exclusivity |
| Accent | `#C9A84C` | Rich gold — prestige, achievement |
| Neutral | `#6B7280` | Cool grey — precision, control |

**Silhouette:** Tall, upright, commanding. Costume adds visual weight and formality. Distinctive feature: the Ruler's silhouette is symmetrical — they present themselves as a formal composition, not a casual one.

**Proportion guidance:** 8–9 head heights (heroic to regal). Upright posture adds apparent height. Costume elements (crown, epaulettes, formal collar) extend the vertical line.

**Costume direction:** Formal, structured, symbolic of their authority. Insignia, heraldic elements, materials that signal status. Condition: immaculate — the Ruler's appearance is a political statement.

**Movement quality:** Controlled, deliberate, economical. The Ruler does not hurry. Every gesture is considered. Stillness communicates power — the Ruler does not need to perform.

**Example characters:** Mufasa, Queen Elizabeth I, Tywin Lannister, Miranda Priestly

**AI prompt hints:** "symmetrical formal silhouette, deep navy and gold, heraldic costume elements, upright commanding posture" / "regal proportions, structured costume with insignia, cool authoritative palette, controlled stillness"

## 3. Archetype Blending Rules

Most compelling characters carry a primary archetype and a secondary that adds psychological complexity. The secondary archetype should deepen the primary, not compete with it.

**Visual blending principle:** The primary archetype governs silhouette and color. The secondary archetype influences costume details, movement quality, and expressive range. Never split the palette equally between two archetype families — it reads as incoherent.

### Compatible Pairings

| Primary | Secondary | Visual Result | Example Characters |
|---------|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Hero | Outlaw | Angular forms with asymmetric wear; warm red with dark accents | Wolverine, Mad Max |
| Hero | Caregiver | Strong silhouette softened by open posture; protective rather than aggressive | Captain America, Aragorn |
| Sage | Ruler | Grounded authority; earth tones with formal structure | Dumbledore, Obi-Wan |
| Explorer | Outlaw | Lean silhouette with deliberately broken elements; earthy palette with dark accents | Han Solo, Jack Sparrow |
| Magician | Creator | Flowing forms with craft tools integrated; purple with vivid accent | Tony Stark, Hephaestus |
| Jester | Outlaw | Chaotic color with sharp triangular elements; yellow with near-black | Harley Quinn, Deadpool |
| Innocent | Caregiver | Pure circles with embracing posture; cream with soft green | Cinderella, Baymax |
| Everyman | Hero | Realistic proportions with subtle heroic elements emerging; blue with warm red accent | Peter Parker, Frodo |

### Incompatible Pairings

| Pairing | Why It Conflicts |
|---------|-----------------|
| Ruler + Jester | Symmetrical authority and chaotic asymmetry cancel each other |
| Innocent + Outlaw | Pure circles and disruptive triangles create cognitive dissonance unless subversion is the explicit intent |
| Caregiver + Outlaw | Embracing protective forms and threatening angular forms pull the silhouette in irreconcilable directions |
| Sage + Lover | Earth tones and warm sensual curves dilute both archetypes; the character reads as neither wise nor passionate |

### How Blending Affects Visual Design

Apply this hierarchy: **silhouette** belongs to the primary archetype only. **Color palette** is governed by the primary, with the secondary contributing one accent color. **Costume details** carry the secondary archetype's signal (a rebel's torn edge on a hero's armor; a caregiver's healing kit on a sage's robes). **Movement quality** blends both — this is where psychological complexity lives.

## 4. Archetype Subversion

Subversion assigns a character the visual language of one archetype while their psychology belongs to another. Use it when the character's deception, hidden nature, or arc is central to the story — when the contrast between appearance and reality is the point.

### Subversion Patterns

| Visual Archetype | True Archetype | Effect | Example |
|-----------------|----------------|--------|---------|
| Innocent (circles, pastels) | Outlaw or Shadow | Deceptive warmth; the threat hidden in softness | Dolores Umbridge, Amy Dunne |
| Caregiver (soft green, open posture) | Ruler | Control disguised as nurturing; manipulation through apparent selflessness | Nurse Ratched |
| Sage (earth tones, grounded) | Magician | Hidden power beneath apparent wisdom; the mentor who is also the most dangerous person in the room | Palpatine (early appearances) |
| Everyman (realistic proportions, plain costume) | Hero | The ordinary person who contains extraordinary capacity; the arc is the revelation | Frodo, Neo |
| Ruler (formal, symmetrical) | Outlaw | Authority as performance; the revolutionary wearing the establishment's clothes | V, Zorro |

### How Subversion Creates Narrative Tension

Audiences read visual archetypes instinctively. When behavior contradicts the visual signal, the audience experiences productive dissonance and must revise their understanding. Design subversion with precision: one element out of register is more effective than wholesale contradiction — a villain with a circle-dominant face but triangle-dominant costume creates unease without confusion. Subversion should be discoverable in retrospect; plant the contradicting element early so it reads as inevitable on rewatch. Movement quality is the hardest element to subvert: a character designed as Innocent but moving with Ruler control will read as wrong before the audience can name why.

## 5. Cast-Level Design

Individual archetype coherence is necessary but not sufficient. Characters must also read as a coherent cast — visually distinct from each other while belonging to the same world.

### Archetype Relationships Across a Lineup

| Relationship | Visual Strategy | Example Pairing |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Complementary | Opposite shape families; contrasting color temperatures | Hero (warm squares) + Sage (cool squares) |
| Contrasting | Opposite silhouette weights; opposing color values | Ruler (tall, dark) + Innocent (small, light) |
| Foil | Same archetype, opposite expression | Two Heroes: one warm red, one cold blue |
| Mirror | Same visual register, different archetype | Caregiver + Innocent: both circular, both warm |

### Silhouette Readability in a Group

At thumbnail scale, each character must be identifiable by silhouette alone. Distribute across weight classes (small/light, medium/grounded, tall/imposing, wide/powerful) — no two characters share the same class. Ensure at least one triangle-dominant, one square-dominant, and one circle-dominant character in any cast. Design costume as part of the silhouette, not decoration on top of it. Test the full cast in grayscale: if two characters merge, adjust value contrast before adjusting hue.

### Complementary Pairs for Visual Variety

| Pair | Visual Contrast | Narrative Function |
|------|----------------|-------------------|
| Hero + Sage | Angular vs. settled/layered | Action and wisdom in dialogue |
| Explorer + Ruler | Asymmetric/lean vs. symmetrical/imposing | Freedom vs. order |
| Jester + Ruler | Chaotic/colorful vs. formal/restrained | The fool who speaks truth to power |
| Innocent + Outlaw | Circular/soft vs. angular/dark | Purity threatened by disruption |
| Lover + Sage | Flowing/warm vs. grounded/cool | Heart and mind in tension |

### Cast Palette Coherence

Establish a world palette — 2–3 colors shared across all characters — and allow each character's archetype palette to operate within that constraint. The world palette creates visual unity; the archetype palette creates individual identity. In a cold, authoritarian world with shared desaturated blues and greys, the Hero's red reads as courage, the Innocent's cream reads as fragility, and the Outlaw's acid green reads as contamination. The same accent colors carry different meaning depending on the ground they sit against.
