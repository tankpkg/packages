# Archetype Visual Identity Map

Sources: Mark & Pearson (The Hero and the Outlaw), Jung (Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious), Adams (Color Design Workbook), Lupton (Thinking with Type)

## The Archetype Wheel

The 12 archetypes organize into four quadrants based on the primary human motivation they address. Each quadrant carries shared visual tendencies that inform the archetypes within it.

| Quadrant | Core Motivation | Archetypes | Visual Register |
|----------|----------------|------------|-----------------|
| **Belonging** | Connection, community, love | Everyman, Lover, Jester, Caregiver | Warm palettes, rounded forms, approachable type |
| **Independence** | Freedom, mastery, self-expression | Explorer, Creator, Hero, Outlaw | Earthy or high-contrast palettes, dynamic shapes, strong type |
| **Stability** | Safety, order, control | Ruler, Caregiver, Sage, Innocent | Structured layouts, authoritative or gentle type, restrained palettes |
| **Change** | Transformation, magic, disruption | Magician, Creator, Explorer, Outlaw | Expressive palettes, unexpected shapes, distinctive type |

**Quadrant visual logic:** Belonging archetypes favor warmth and accessibility. Independence archetypes favor movement and authenticity. Stability archetypes favor structure and permanence. Change archetypes favor surprise and transformation. Archetypes at quadrant intersections — Caregiver (Belonging/Stability), Creator (Independence/Change) — draw from both registers.

## The 12 Archetypes

---

### 1. The Innocent

**Core desire:** Safety, simplicity, happiness  
**Brand promise:** We offer goodness without complication

**Color palette**

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#F5F0E8` | Warm cream — purity without clinical coldness |
| Secondary | `#B8D4E8` | Soft sky blue — openness, calm |
| Accent | `#E8C87A` | Warm gold — gentle optimism |
| Supporting | `#D4EAD0` | Mint — freshness, nature |

Avoid saturated or dark colors. The palette should feel like morning light, not a hospital.

**Shape language:** Rounded corners, soft organic curves, open negative space. No sharp angles. Circles and gentle ovals. Layouts breathe — generous white space signals simplicity.

**Typography direction:** Rounded humanist sans-serifs or gentle transitional serifs. Light to regular weights. Open letterforms with generous x-height. Avoid condensed or heavy weights entirely.
- Display: Nunito (rounded, warm) or Lato Light
- Body: Open Sans Regular or Source Sans Pro

**Imagery style:** Natural daylight, outdoor settings, unposed moments. Children, families, nature. Minimal post-processing — images should feel honest and unfiltered.

**Brand examples:** Dove, Innocent Drinks, Johnson & Johnson, Aveeno, Coca-Cola (nostalgia campaigns)

**SVG prompt hint:** "clean minimal logo, soft rounded letterforms, warm cream and sky blue, gentle curves, open airy composition, no sharp edges"

### 2. The Explorer

**Core desire:** Freedom, discovery, authentic experience  
**Brand promise:** We help you find your own path

**Color palette**

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#8B4513` | Terracotta — earth, adventure, warmth |
| Secondary | `#2D5016` | Forest green — nature, endurance |
| Accent | `#C4622D` | Burnt orange — energy, movement |
| Supporting | `#C4A882` | Sand — openness, horizon |

Palettes should feel like a landscape photograph — natural, varied, grounded. Avoid corporate blues and synthetic brights.

**Shape language:** Horizontal lines suggesting movement and open horizon. Asymmetric layouts. Topographic contours, compass roses, path motifs. Rough or hand-drawn edges signal authenticity over polish.

**Typography direction:** Sturdy, condensed sans-serifs or rugged slab serifs. Medium to bold weights. Slightly weathered or utilitarian feel — built for function, not decoration.
- Display: Oswald Bold or Roboto Condensed Bold
- Body: Source Sans Pro or Work Sans

**Imagery style:** Wide landscapes, open roads, wilderness, physical movement. Authentic over staged. Grain and texture welcome. People shown in motion or in nature, not in studios.

**Brand examples:** Patagonia, REI, Jeep, The North Face, National Geographic, Timberland, Subaru

**SVG prompt hint:** "bold condensed wordmark, terracotta and forest green, horizontal composition suggesting movement, rugged texture, topographic or compass motif"

### 3. The Sage

**Core desire:** Truth, knowledge, understanding  
**Brand promise:** We give you the insight to decide wisely

**Color palette**

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#1B3A6B` | Deep navy — authority, depth, trust |
| Secondary | `#2E6B5E` | Deep teal — intelligence, clarity |
| Accent | `#C9A84C` | Warm gold — wisdom, credibility |
| Supporting | `#6B6B6B` | Charcoal grey — neutrality, precision |

Palettes should feel like a well-designed academic journal — authoritative without being cold, credible without being corporate.

**Shape language:** Clean geometric forms, structured grids, academic proportions. Strong horizontal and vertical alignment. Minimal decorative elements — structure carries the meaning.

**Typography direction:** Classic transitional or old-style serifs for authority; clean geometric sans-serifs for modern Sage brands. Strong typographic hierarchy is essential — the Sage communicates through information architecture.
- Display: Playfair Display or Libre Baskerville
- Body: Merriweather or Source Serif Pro
- Modern variant: Inter (heading) + Source Serif Pro (body)

**Imagery style:** Data visualizations, clean architecture, thoughtful people in context. Books, research environments, precise tools. Photography should feel considered and purposeful, not decorative.

**Brand examples:** Google, BBC, The Economist, Harvard, TED, McKinsey, Audi

**SVG prompt hint:** "structured geometric logo, deep navy and gold, strong typographic hierarchy, clean grid-based composition, authoritative serif or precise sans-serif"

### 4. The Hero

**Core desire:** Mastery, achievement, proving worth through action  
**Brand promise:** We give you the tools to overcome any challenge

**Color palette**

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#CC0000` | Bold red — courage, urgency, power |
| Secondary | `#0A1628` | Deep navy — discipline, strength |
| Accent | `#FF6B00` | Strong orange — energy, drive |
| Supporting | `#1A1A1A` | Near black — intensity, focus |

High contrast is non-negotiable. The Hero palette should feel like it demands something of the viewer.

**Shape language:** Dynamic diagonals, upward-pointing forms, bold geometric shapes. Angular, not rounded. Compositions suggest forward momentum. No decorative softness.

**Typography direction:** Bold condensed sans-serifs. Maximum weight contrast between display and body. Powerful, no-nonsense letterforms. Condensed display fonts amplify the sense of compressed energy.
- Display: Barlow Condensed ExtraBold or Bebas Neue
- Body: Barlow or Roboto Medium

**Imagery style:** Athletes at peak effort, physical achievement, overcoming obstacles. High contrast photography. Motion blur, dramatic lighting. The subject is always in action, never passive.

**Brand examples:** Nike, Adidas, FedEx, BMW, Gatorade, Duracell, US Army

**SVG prompt hint:** "bold condensed logo, high contrast red and black, dynamic diagonal composition, angular geometric forms, strong upward movement"

---

### 5. The Outlaw

**Core desire:** Revolution, disruption, freedom from imposed rules  
**Brand promise:** We reject the system that holds you back

**Color palette**

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#0D0D0D` | Near black — defiance, raw power |
| Secondary | `#8B0000` | Deep red — danger, passion, rebellion |
| Accent | `#CCFF00` | Acid yellow-green — provocation, disruption |
| Supporting | `#4A4A4A` | Raw grey — industrial, unfinished |

Monochromatic black-and-white with a single disruptive accent is a strong Outlaw move. Avoid anything that reads as polished or corporate.

**Shape language:** Broken forms, distressed textures, asymmetry, raw edges. Grunge aesthetics used deliberately. Layouts that violate conventional grid logic. Negative space used aggressively.

**Typography direction:** Bold, distressed, or brutalist typefaces. Unconventional layouts — type at unexpected angles, oversized letterforms, deliberate rule-breaking. Hand-drawn or stencil aesthetics.
- Display: Bebas Neue or custom distressed display
- Body: Space Grotesk or DM Mono (monospace adds anti-establishment feel)

**Imagery style:** Counter-culture, raw authenticity, anti-establishment imagery. Gritty photography, high contrast, unretouched. Subjects that challenge conventions.

**Brand examples:** Harley-Davidson, Virgin, Diesel, Converse, Liquid Death, MTV

**SVG prompt hint:** "bold distressed logo, black and acid yellow, asymmetric composition, raw edges, stencil or grunge texture, anti-establishment energy"

### 6. The Magician

**Core desire:** Transformation, making the impossible real  
**Brand promise:** We turn your vision into reality

**Color palette**

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#2D1B69` | Deep purple — mystery, transformation |
| Secondary | `#0D1B3E` | Midnight blue — depth, possibility |
| Accent | `#C9A84C` | Gold — achievement, wonder |
| Supporting | `#1A0A2E` | Near black with violet undertone — depth |

Iridescent and holographic treatments work for Magician brands in digital contexts. Jewel tones — emerald, sapphire, amethyst — are all valid. The palette should feel like it contains hidden depth.

**Shape language:** Circular forms (transformation, cycles), spirals, stars, sacred geometry. Shapes that suggest hidden meaning or dual readings. Negative space used to create revelation moments.

**Typography direction:** Elegant, slightly mysterious. High-contrast serifs with dramatic thick-thin variation, or refined display fonts with distinctive character. Script accents used sparingly for wonder.
- Display: Cormorant Garamond or Cinzel
- Body: Montserrat Light or Raleway

**Imagery style:** Transformation sequences, before/after, technology as magic. Wonder and revelation. Dramatic lighting — single light sources, deep shadows. The moment of change.

**Brand examples:** Disney, Apple, Dyson, Tesla, Mastercard, Polaroid

**SVG prompt hint:** "elegant mysterious logo, deep purple and gold, circular or spiral motif, high-contrast serif letterforms, sense of hidden depth and transformation"

### 7. The Lover

**Core desire:** Intimacy, connection, sensory pleasure  
**Brand promise:** We celebrate beauty and the pleasure of being alive

**Color palette**

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#8B1A3A` | Deep rose-burgundy — passion, depth |
| Secondary | `#F2C4CE` | Blush pink — softness, intimacy |
| Accent | `#C9A87A` | Champagne gold — luxury, warmth |
| Supporting | `#FAF0E6` | Warm cream — sensuality, comfort |

The Lover palette should feel warm and tactile. Avoid cool blues and clinical whites. Rich, warm, and inviting.

**Shape language:** Curves, flowing forms, organic shapes, sensual lines. Nothing angular or rigid. Compositions that feel embracing rather than confrontational. Generous use of negative space to create intimacy.

**Typography direction:** Elegant scripts for display, refined high-contrast serifs for headings. Graceful letterforms with visible calligraphic influence. Light weights for body text — the Lover whispers, not shouts.
- Display: Cormorant Garamond Italic or a refined script
- Body: EB Garamond or Libre Baskerville Light

**Imagery style:** Intimacy, beauty, sensory detail — texture of fabric, steam from coffee, close-up skin. Warm, soft lighting. Couples or solitary figures in moments of pleasure. Luxury details.

**Brand examples:** L'Oreal, Chanel, Victoria's Secret, Godiva, Haagen-Dazs, Alfa Romeo, Hallmark

**SVG prompt hint:** "elegant flowing logo, deep rose and champagne gold, curved organic letterforms, high-contrast serif, warm intimate composition, sensual negative space"

### 8. The Jester

**Core desire:** Fun, play, joy, living fully in the present  
**Brand promise:** We make the ordinary delightful

**Color palette**

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#FFD700` | Bright yellow — joy, energy, optimism |
| Secondary | `#FF4500` | Vivid orange-red — excitement, fun |
| Accent | `#00BFFF` | Electric blue — surprise, playfulness |
| Supporting | `#FFFFFF` | White — clarity, bounce |

Saturated, high-energy combinations. The Jester palette should feel like it's having fun. Multiple bright colors used together are appropriate — the Jester breaks the "limit your palette" rule deliberately.

**Shape language:** Playful, irregular, bouncy. Rounded corners everywhere. Unexpected compositions, tilted elements, overlapping forms. Layouts that feel spontaneous rather than planned.

**Typography direction:** Playful, rounded, quirky. Variable weights used expressively. Hand-drawn elements welcome. Expressive letterforms that feel like they're enjoying themselves.
- Display: Nunito ExtraBold or Fredoka One
- Body: Nunito or Poppins

**Imagery style:** Humor, unexpected juxtapositions, laughter, irreverence. Bright, saturated photography. People caught in genuine moments of delight. Illustration and animation welcome.

**Brand examples:** M&Ms, Old Spice, Dollar Shave Club, Skittles, Ben & Jerry's, Mailchimp, Wendy's

**SVG prompt hint:** "playful rounded logo, bright yellow and electric blue, bouncy irregular letterforms, tilted or unexpected composition, energetic and fun"

### 9. The Everyman

**Core desire:** Belonging, connection, being part of something real  
**Brand promise:** We are for everyone, without pretension

**Color palette**

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#3A6EA5` | Mid blue — honest, reliable, approachable |
| Secondary | `#8B7355` | Warm brown — authenticity, groundedness |
| Accent | `#E87722` | Friendly orange — warmth, accessibility |
| Supporting | `#F5F0E8` | Warm off-white — unpretentious, welcoming |

Nothing too flashy, nothing too refined. The Everyman palette should feel like it belongs to everyone — not aspirational, not exclusive.

**Shape language:** Familiar, approachable, nothing avant-garde. Solid, reliable forms. Conventional grid layouts that feel comfortable rather than surprising. Rounded but not excessively so.

**Typography direction:** Friendly, highly readable sans-serifs. Approachable weights — regular to medium, never ultra-light or ultra-heavy. Nothing too stylized. The type should feel like it's talking to you, not performing.
- Display: Lato Bold or Roboto Bold
- Body: Open Sans or Lato Regular

**Imagery style:** Real people in everyday moments. Community, shared experience, authenticity. Unretouched or lightly processed photography. Diverse, representative, unpretentious.

**Brand examples:** IKEA, Target, Levi's, Home Depot, Budweiser, Gap, eBay

**SVG prompt hint:** "clean approachable logo, mid blue and warm orange, friendly sans-serif letterforms, solid reliable composition, unpretentious and accessible"

### 10. The Caregiver

**Core desire:** Protecting and nurturing others  
**Brand promise:** We are here for you, unconditionally

**Color palette**

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#4A9B6F` | Soft green — growth, healing, care |
| Secondary | `#5B8DB8` | Warm blue — calm, trust, protection |
| Accent | `#E8A598` | Gentle pink — warmth, compassion |
| Supporting | `#FAF5EE` | Warm cream — comfort, safety |

The Caregiver palette should feel like a warm embrace. Avoid anything clinical, cold, or aggressive. Muted, gentle versions of colors rather than saturated primaries.

**Shape language:** Soft, rounded, embracing forms. Protective enclosures — circles and arcs that wrap around content. Gentle curves. Layouts that feel safe and organized without being rigid.

**Typography direction:** Warm, readable, approachable. Humanist sans-serifs or gentle serifs. Never cold or clinical. Medium weights — the Caregiver is present and clear, not whispering or shouting.
- Display: Nunito SemiBold or Lato Bold
- Body: Open Sans or Merriweather (for healthcare contexts)

**Imagery style:** Families, care relationships, warmth, healing. Soft natural lighting. Genuine emotional moments — not stock-photo perfection. Hands, touch, presence.

**Brand examples:** Johnson & Johnson, UNICEF, Pampers, Campbell's Soup, Volvo, WWF

**SVG prompt hint:** "warm rounded logo, soft green and gentle blue, embracing circular forms, humanist sans-serif, nurturing and protective composition"

### 11. The Ruler

**Core desire:** Control, order, prosperity, leadership  
**Brand promise:** We set the standard that others follow

**Color palette**

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#0A1628` | Deep navy — authority, permanence |
| Secondary | `#1A1A1A` | Near black — power, exclusivity |
| Accent | `#C9A84C` | Rich gold — prestige, achievement |
| Supporting | `#6B7280` | Cool grey — precision, control |

The Ruler palette should feel expensive and authoritative. Deep, rich, and restrained. Gold used as accent, never as primary — overuse of gold reads as gaudy, not prestigious.

**Shape language:** Structured, symmetrical, formal. Strong geometric forms. Heraldic elements — crests, shields, formal borders. Layouts with clear hierarchy and generous white space that signals confidence.

**Typography direction:** Classic serifs with authority. Strong typographic hierarchy. Formal spacing — generous tracking in display text signals confidence and exclusivity. Never casual or rounded.
- Display: Trajan Pro or Cinzel (for heritage Ruler brands)
- Display: Didot or Bodoni (for fashion/luxury Ruler brands)
- Body: Garamond or EB Garamond

**Imagery style:** Power, prestige, excellence, craftsmanship. Precision photography — sharp focus, controlled lighting. Premium materials, architectural spaces, leadership contexts.

**Brand examples:** Rolex, Mercedes-Benz, American Express, Louis Vuitton, Rolls-Royce, Barclays

**SVG prompt hint:** "authoritative formal logo, deep navy and gold, symmetrical heraldic composition, classic serif letterforms, generous spacing, premium and controlled"

### 12. The Creator

**Core desire:** Creating something of enduring value and originality  
**Brand promise:** We give you the tools and space to make something real

**Color palette**

The Creator has no fixed palette — its visual identity is defined by distinctiveness and intentionality rather than a specific color family. Two common Creator expressions:

*Expressive Creator (Adobe, Crayola):*

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#FF0000` | Pure red — bold, primary, foundational |
| Secondary | `#0000FF` | Pure blue — contrast, possibility |
| Accent | `#FFD700` | Yellow — creativity, light |

*Refined Creator (Apple, Moleskine):*

| Role | Hex | Description |
|------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | `#1A1A1A` | Near black — craft, precision |
| Secondary | `#F5F5F5` | Near white — space, possibility |
| Accent | `#0071E3` | Precise blue — innovation |

**Shape language:** Expressive, original, often asymmetric. Celebrates the handmade and unique. Can range from geometric precision (Apple) to organic expressiveness (Etsy). The defining quality is intentionality — every shape choice is deliberate.

**Typography direction:** Distinctive, often custom or highly curated. Expressive display fonts that feel chosen rather than defaulted to. The Creator's type should feel like a design decision, not a template.
- Expressive: Syne or Abril Fatface
- Refined: SF Pro (Apple) or Canela (editorial)
- Accessible: Josefin Sans + Work Sans

**Imagery style:** Making, craft, process, originality. Tools, materials, work-in-progress. The act of creation as subject. Can be highly polished (Apple) or deliberately raw (Etsy).

**Brand examples:** Apple, Adobe, LEGO, Crayola, Pinterest, Etsy, Moleskine, Canva

**SVG prompt hint:** "distinctive expressive logo, bold primary colors or refined monochrome, asymmetric or precisely geometric composition, custom-feeling letterforms, celebrates craft and originality"

## Archetype Blending Rules

Most brands carry a primary archetype and a secondary that adds nuance. The secondary should never compete with the primary — it deepens it.

### Compatible Pairings

| Primary | Compatible Secondary | Why It Works |
|---------|---------------------|--------------|
| Hero | Outlaw | Adds edge and authenticity to achievement (Under Armour) |
| Hero | Ruler | Adds authority and prestige to performance (BMW) |
| Explorer | Outlaw | Adds counter-culture credibility to adventure (Patagonia) |
| Explorer | Creator | Adds originality to discovery (National Geographic) |
| Sage | Ruler | Adds authority to expertise (Harvard, McKinsey) |
| Sage | Creator | Adds innovation to knowledge (Google, TED) |
| Magician | Creator | Adds craft to transformation (Apple) |
| Magician | Ruler | Adds exclusivity to wonder (Disney premium) |
| Lover | Caregiver | Adds warmth to sensuality (Hallmark) |
| Jester | Everyman | Adds accessibility to humor (Ben & Jerry's) |
| Caregiver | Innocent | Adds purity to nurturing (Dove) |
| Innocent | Caregiver | Adds protection to simplicity (Johnson & Johnson) |

### Conflicting Pairings

| Pairing | Why It Conflicts |
|---------|-----------------|
| Hero + Caregiver | Achievement and nurturing pull in opposite directions visually and emotionally |
| Ruler + Jester | Authority and irreverence cancel each other out |
| Outlaw + Caregiver | Rebellion and protection are fundamentally incompatible |
| Innocent + Outlaw | Purity and disruption create cognitive dissonance |
| Sage + Jester | Seriousness and playfulness dilute each other unless executed with extreme precision |

**Blending visual rule:** When combining archetypes, the primary archetype governs color and shape. The secondary archetype influences typography and imagery tone. Never split the palette between two archetype families — it reads as inconsistent.

## Archetype-to-Industry Fit

| Industry | Primary Fit | Secondary Fit | Avoid |
|----------|-------------|---------------|-------|
| Financial services | Ruler, Sage | Caregiver (retail banking) | Jester, Outlaw |
| Healthcare | Caregiver | Innocent, Sage | Outlaw, Jester |
| Technology (enterprise) | Sage, Ruler | Magician | Jester |
| Technology (consumer) | Magician, Creator | Explorer, Jester | Ruler |
| Luxury goods | Ruler, Lover | Magician | Everyman, Jester |
| Outdoor / adventure | Explorer | Outlaw, Hero | Ruler, Lover |
| Food & beverage (mass) | Jester, Everyman | Innocent | Ruler |
| Food & beverage (premium) | Lover, Ruler | Magician | Jester |
| Fitness / sports | Hero | Outlaw, Explorer | Caregiver |
| Education | Sage | Caregiver, Creator | Outlaw |
| Nonprofit / charity | Caregiver | Innocent, Everyman | Ruler, Outlaw |
| Fashion (luxury) | Lover, Ruler | Magician | Everyman |
| Fashion (streetwear) | Outlaw, Hero | Jester | Ruler |
| Children's products | Innocent, Jester | Caregiver | Outlaw, Ruler |
| Creative tools / software | Creator | Magician, Explorer | Ruler |
| Automotive (premium) | Ruler, Hero | Magician | Jester |
| Automotive (adventure) | Explorer | Outlaw, Hero | Ruler |

**Industry fit is a starting point, not a constraint.** Differentiation often comes from choosing the underserved archetype in a category — a financial services brand that leads with Caregiver or a fitness brand that leads with Jester creates immediate distinction precisely because competitors cluster around the expected archetype.
